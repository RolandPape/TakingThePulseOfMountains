Cold Fading.

Life Narrowing.

Mountains Warning.

Up here, the cold once shaped everything. Now the mountains are warming faster than the valleys below, reshuffling snow, water, and life itself.

Alpine ecosystems are simple by design — few species, delicate balances, narrow margins. That simplicity is their beauty, and their weakness.

Yet within this simplicity lies surprising richness: the rugged topography fractures the landscape into various microhabitats creating high ecological diversity over very short distances.

As the climate warms, species climb toward the sky until the ridgelines stop them. Some may disappear as their world shrinks and they run out of mountain.

The mountains are changing — and they are telling us what’s coming.

    The Project's Rationale & Objectives

    In Norway, vast stretches of the country rise into the alpine zone — home to many specialised and threatened species and nature types. These high mountains form some of our most climate sensitive environments, giving us a special responsibility to understand, protect, and restore them as change accelerates.

    What started in winter 2008 as a monitoring of reindeer's habitat use and pasture resources, evolved over the years into a full-scale monitoring of the alpine landscape and treeline at Filefjell, located in south-central Norway.

    Using Filefjell as an example, we seek to

    i) understand current (sub)alpine ecosystem functioning and

    ii) to track changes in ecological patterns and processes over time.

    Study Design

    The monitoring program covers both tree line and - evenly distributed across the low and middle alpine belt above tree line - topographical gradients from the hilltops into snowbeds. Here, we conduct remote sensing (from drone to satellite) aided vegetation and snowcover mapping, have established permanent vegetation plots, monitor near-surface temperature conditions, conduct climate-growth analyses of woody species based on dendroecology, and look into the properties and characteristics of alpine soils.
